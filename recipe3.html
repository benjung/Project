Recipe #3: Stir-fried Baby Octopus

Step 1: Cut open the heads and remove the intestines and beaks.
Step 2: Put in a large bowl. Add flour and scrub vigorously for about 2 minutes until it foams. This process not only tenderizes the octopus but also cleans any dirt or mud stuck in the suckers.
Step 3: Rinse in cold running water until itâ€™s not slippery anymore. Drain.
Step 4: Bring a pot of water to a boil. Blanch the octopus for 10 to 12 seconds, drain, and then transfer to a cutting board.
Step 5: Cut the heads and arms into bite size pieces. Set aside.
Step 6: Combine seasoning ingredients (hot pepper flakes, soy sauce, sugar, potato starch, rice syrup, ground black pepper) in a bowl. Mix well with a spoon.
Step 7: Heat up a large pan over high heat. Add vegetable oil. Stir in garlic, ginger, green chili pepper, onion, and green onion for about 3 minutes until the garlic turns a little brown and the onion translucent.
Step 8: Add octopus and seasoning sauce. Stir for 2 to 3 minutes.
Step 9: Remove from the heat and stir in sesame oil. Transfer it to a serving plate and serve right away with rice.
